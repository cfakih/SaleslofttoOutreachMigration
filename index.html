Common Questions

How long does a typical migration take and what factors affect timeline?
Timeline ranges from 6-10 weeks depending on team size and complexity. Teams under 100 users typically complete in 6 weeks, while organizations with 200+ users need 8-10 weeks. Key factors include content volume, regional coordination needs, custom automation requirements, and training schedule coordination around business cycles.
What happens to prospects currently in Salesloft cadences?
We offer three approaches: (1) Natural completion - stop new additions and let existing cadences finish; (2) Placeholder sequences - transfer prospects to generic follow-up sequences in Outreach; (3) Mapped migration - recreate equivalent sequences and transfer at appropriate steps. Most customers choose option 1 or 2 for simplicity.
How much time will our team need to invest in the migration?
For most implementations, expect 3-5 hours weekly from your project lead, with concentrated efforts during specific phases. CRM admins need 8-15 hours total for integration setup. Content creators require 10-25 hours depending on migration volume. End users need just 2 hours of training time.
Can we accelerate the timeline if needed?
Yes, Outreach won't be the bottleneck. Acceleration depends on your team's resource availability and decision-making speed. We can compress phases and run parallel workstreams when your team can support faster execution.
What about training timing - too early and people forget, too late and they scramble?
We've refined the training schedule through hundreds of implementations. Managers train first, then end users train 2-3 days before go-live. This ensures information is fresh while providing adequate preparation time. We coordinate around your business cycles to avoid disruption during critical periods.
How do we ensure no business disruption during the switch?
Zero-downtime transitions are our standard approach. Technical setup happens in parallel with your existing operations. The actual switch occurs over a weekend, with comprehensive testing and rollback procedures. Teams continue prospecting seamlessly from Monday morning.
What if our RevOps team is already at 120% utilization?
This is common - most RevOps teams are stretched thin. Our project managers work specifically with resource-constrained teams. We design implementations around your availability, provide clear time blocks rather than continuous commitment, and can adjust phases based on your bandwidth.
How is the Salesforce integration different from Salesloft?
Major improvements include: single integration user (eliminates individual authentication issues), bi-directional sync with conflict resolution (retries failed syncs up to 6 times), bulk API usage for efficiency, and automatic ownership respect without data corruption. These architectural differences eliminate common Salesloft sync problems.
